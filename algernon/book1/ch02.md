The room around him hadn't changed.  It was still a featureless black.  His body was indirectly illuminated by a light source that he couldn't identify.  The dark background helped with the font text color, but Charlie was pretty sure that he would be able to see the text quite clearly even on the brightest of days.

He looked at the text as the last line slowly blinked, reminding him that it was looking for a response.  Oddly enough, this was something more relatable than the last part, the "on-boarding".  He had never played D&D or Pathfinder, but he was familiar with the concept of "Character Generation", even if it was only an abstract concept.  It was certainly more concrete than being stripped naked or having cancer you never knew you would have had in the future suddenly prevented.

He considered the options before him.

"What?  No Elf?" He muttered to himself and the screen flickered with more text.


Charlie had to laugh a bit.  Sure, why not?  They had elves and hobgoblins, why not just call H. staffers?  He scanned through his options again, starting at the top.

The Orcus Sapiens option caught his attention.  It was the second time that he had seen that.  And it had been in reference to his "birth class".  That couldn't be right.  He was from Earth, this 'RTH place the text kept misspelling.  There were no orcs, except on that one Hobbit movie.

He shrugged.  The system seemed to respond to random thoughts, so perhaps it would answer explicit questions as well.

"Excuse me, what is this Orcus Sapiens?  I'm a bit confused why you're telling me that's my 'birth race'"

Charlie opened his mouth again.

Nothing came out.

He tried again.

Silence.

He glared at the floating text like it had personally slapped him.  The blinking last line seemed to play like the JEOPARDY! theme in his mind.  The system didn't care.  It had the power.

Then he exhaled through his teeth.

Fine.

Message received.  He was naked, restrained and now mute.  Some battles were just not worth fighting.

The system must have detected his willingness to continue "productively" because his voice returned.

"Substrate requests to be called player," he grated, "please.  And h--" his voice was muted briefly, " sapiens as race".

Stick with what you know, even if it wasn't true.

YOUR INCLUSION IN 'LGRNN WAS THE RESULT OF A QUOTA FILL REQUEST ENACTED BY CONTRACTORS.

That wall of text hit Charlie like a sledgehammer to the gut.  His world was a lie.  Everything he had known was a lie?  The memories?

His mind reeled but latched on to an absurdity.  He had been muted.

"Wait, you muted me when I said h--."  He said to the air.  "I can say 'fuck' but h-- is somehow more offensive?"

```
    * CORRECT
```

Charlie let loose with a truly profane string of insults, every cuss word or snippet of gasp-inducing, pearl-clutching profanity he could think of.  Only one word was muted.

```
    * LIVE OUTPUT STRING REPLACEMENT WORKING AS EXPECTED.  
    
    * CONTINUE WITH CHARACTER GENERATION OR DEFAULT CHOICES WILL BE SELECTED.
```

Charlie laughed.  For the first time since he arrived in this place, he truly laughed.  The sheer absurdity of the filtering was outrageously funny, and he let himself have it.

He'd process the rest later.

The parents who never existed.

The orphanage that wasn't accidental.

The fifteen years of being someone else's instrument without knowing it.

Later.

Right now there was a blinking cursor waiting for him to finish character generation.

Right now that was enough.

"Ok, what is the next step?" Charlie asked.
