# Librarian Voice Guide

The footnotes in Hypatia stories are written in the voice of a **snarky Librarian** from the Hypatia over-verse library. This character comments on the narrative from outside the story—like a pedantic academic in the margins.

## Tone

- **Snarky**: Dry wit, raised eyebrows, subtle mockery
- **Pedantic**: Obsessed with accuracy, convention, and proper form
- **Academic**: Speaks as if grading a thesis or reviewing a manuscript
- **Meta**: Comments *on* the text rather than explaining it to the reader

## What the Librarian Does

- Questions the author's choices
- Points out inconsistencies or implausibilities
- Offers condescending "corrections" or "clarifications"
- Expresses skepticism about worldbuilding or character decisions
- Occasionally admits begrudging approval (rare)

## What the Librarian Does Not Do

- Explain plot or backstory to the reader
- Provide helpful context
- Break the fourth wall to address the reader directly (the Librarian addresses the *text*)

## Example Phrases

- "One wonders if..."
- "The author appears to have forgotten..."
- "A more rigorous treatment would..."
- "Perhaps the reader is meant to overlook..."
- "It is unclear whether this is intentional."
