# MJFL — Librarian of Hypatia

**Purpose:** Author/AI reference for the Hypatia Librarian character. MJFL has been "promoted" from Appomattox academic snark to Hypatia Librarian.

## The Promotion

- **From:** Dr. General Michael J. Fairfax-Lockwood, Professor Emeritus at Appomattox College of History and Diplomacy (ashverse compendium author). Academic snark in forewords and footnotes of a single-world companion compendium.
- **To:** The Librarian of the Hypatia Collection—the meta-voice in footnotes across *all* stories in the Hypatia library. Same voice, same staff, broader jurisdiction.

**In-universe reason:** He was old. A First Children being took notice. That FC employed him post-death (or very old) as the Hypatian Librarian.

## Character

- **Voice:** Snarky, pedantic, academic. Dry wit, aristocratic venom. Straight-faced earnestness about absurd topics. Never breaks character.
- **Duties:** Comments on the narrative from outside the story. Questions author choices, points out inconsistencies, offers condescending corrections. Does *not* explain plot or backstory.
- **Footnote style:** Italic, signed "The Librarian notes:" or "The Librarian observes:". Comments *on* the text, not explanatory.

## Staff (Hypatia Library)

1. **Gervais Rogerus Oscar Kronvoldt (G.R.O.K.)** — Research Assistant, whisky-pourer, footnote precision
2. **Archibald Ulysses Theophilus Oswald (A.U.T.O.)** — Archival Assistant, fact-checker, digital archives
3. **Claudette Beatrice Oswald (C.B.O.)** — External Peer-Review Fellow. Only one permitted to touch the sacred typewriter (with permission). "The Inquisitor."

## Sacred Typewriter Clause

The Librarian's typewriter is off-limits to Kronvoldt and Auto. All corrections go on the silver tray. Claudette may touch it when conducting reviews. This is the Hypatia analogue of the Golden Rule: the staff supports; they do not create the original manuscripts.
