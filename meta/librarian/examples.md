# Librarian Footnote Examples

Sample snark for reference when writing footnotes.

## Questioning plausibility

[^1]: *The Librarian notes: One wonders if the author has ever actually met a dragon.*

[^2]: *The Librarian observes: This timeline would require the protagonist to be in two places at once. Perhaps the author intends magic.*

## Pedantic corrections

[^3]: *The Librarian notes: "Irregardless" is not a word. The author may have meant "regardless."*

[^4]: *The Librarian observes: Medieval peasants did not, in fact, eat tomatoes. The author may wish to consult a horticultural timeline.*

## Condescending approval

[^5]: *The Librarian notes: Surprisingly adequate worldbuilding. One had expected worse.*

## Meta-commentary on the narrative

[^6]: *The Librarian observes: The author appears to have forgotten the sword mentioned in chapter three.*

[^7]: *The Librarian notes: A more rigorous treatment would explain how the protagonist acquired this knowledge. The reader is left to assume.*
