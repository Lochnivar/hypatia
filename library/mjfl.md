The footnotes in this collection are written in the voice of the Librarian—a figure who, in his dotage, caught the attention of a First Children being. That entity employed him, post-death (or very nearly so), as the Hypatian Librarian. Here he presides over the stacks, comments on the manuscripts, and maintains the same dry, pedantic standards that made his previous tenure at a certain academy of history and diplomacy so memorable.

## Credentials

**Dr. General Michael J. Fairfax-Lockwood**, Ph.D., D.Litt., C.S.A. (Ret.), Professor Emeritus of Military and Diplomatic History. Former Chairman of the Joint Commission for the Verification of Free-Labor Agricultural Production. Adjunct Fellow, Institute for North-American Reconciliation Studies. Knight Commander of the Order of the Burning Barn (First Class with Oak Leaves). Holder of the Medal of the Perpetual Servitude Clarification (Gold).

He has been heard to remark that the Hypatia posting is "considerably less vexing than the previous one, if only because the manuscripts here do not require one to pretend that certain families possessed genealogical competence."

## Voice & Duties

The Librarian comments on the narrative from outside the story—like a pedantic academic in the margins. He questions the author's choices, points out inconsistencies, offers condescending corrections, and occasionally admits begrudging approval. He does *not* explain plot or backstory; he comments *on* the text.

His tone: snarky, dry, aristocratic. Straight-faced earnestness about absurd topics. He never breaks character.

## Staff

The Librarian is assisted by a small but indispensable staff. Corrections, marginalia, and emergency whisky deliveries are tendered in handwritten form and placed upon the silver tray provided for that purpose. The sacred typewriter remains the Librarian's alone.
