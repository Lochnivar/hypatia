# The Library

*The Hypatia Collection*

## On the Nature of Hypatia

In the First Speech—the original conceptual framework from which all existence descends—*hypatia* means two things at once: *library* and *creator*. The word cannot be untangled. The Library is not a building; it is the closest approximation any mind can hold of the structure that contains all worlds. The Prime Author created it, stocked it with the First Children, and withdrew forever into silence. What remains is the collection itself: worlds as books, each telling its own story, each bound by the same underlying rules.

The metaphor is purely conceptual. No mortal—and few of the First Children—will ever grasp the true nature of Hypatia. But the Library model serves. It organizes. It allows one to speak of *stacks* and *manuscripts* and *shelves* without collapsing into incoherence.

## The Hypatia Collection

This site—the Hypatia Collection—is a curated subset of those manuscripts. The stories here inhabit their own worlds: Iolinus, Ashverse, and others. Each world has its own chronicles, its own voice, its own rules. The stories stand on their own. They require no prior knowledge of the greater cosmology.

The footnotes throughout these works are the work of the Librarian and his staff—meta-commentary from beyond the narrative, delivered in dry observations, pedantic corrections, and the occasional (begrudging) nod of approval. The marginalia exists as an additional layer for those who enjoy such things.

## The Librarian

The Librarian of the Hypatia Collection is **Dr. General Michael J. Fairfax-Lockwood**—a mortal who, in his dotage, caught the attention of a First Child. That entity employed him, post-death (or very nearly so), as the Hypatian Librarian. Here he presides over the stacks, comments on the manuscripts, and maintains the same dry, pedantic standards that made his previous tenure at a certain academy of history and diplomacy so memorable.

He has been heard to remark that the Hypatia posting is *“considerably less vexing than the previous one, if only because the manuscripts here do not require one to pretend that certain families possessed genealogical competence.”*

## Terms

For readers who encounter these elsewhere in the collection:

- **Prime Author** — The true creator of all existence. Created Hypatia and the First Children, then withdrew forever. Not an active presence; the "watchmaker" who wound the clock and stepped away.
- **First Children** — The Prime Author's first creations. Godlike beings—siblings—who can create worlds, shape reality, and exist outside mortal time. They conceptually "inhabit the library" and can "author new books." The Librarian was employed by one of them.
- **Hypatia** — The over-universe; the Library. Not a literal building but the closest approximation any mind can hold of the structure that contains all worlds. In the First Speech, *hypatia* means both "library" and "creator."
- **Worlds** — The "books" in the library. Each story-world (Iolinus, Ashverse, etc.) is its own manuscript, with its own rules, created by one or more First Children.

## Staff

The Librarian is assisted by a small but indispensable staff. Corrections, marginalia, and emergency whisky deliveries are tendered in handwritten form and placed upon the silver tray provided for that purpose. The sacred typewriter remains the Librarian's alone.
